Bagacay Festival: A Celebration of MacArthur’s Heritage
Every year on June 17, the vibrant municipality of MacArthur, Leyte bursts into a colorful celebration known as the Bagacay Festival. This lively event marks the founding anniversary of the town and serves as a heartfelt tribute to its rich historical roots, culture, and community spirit. A Festival Steeped in History and Tradition
The Bagacay Festival is more than just a festivity—it is a symbol of MacArthur’s identity and pride. The name "Bagacay" refers to a native bamboo species traditionally used by locals, symbolizing resilience, strength, and flexibility—qualities that reflect the town’s enduring spirit throughout its history. Through this annual celebration, residents honor their ancestors, commemorate the town’s origins, and showcase the vibrant cultural tapestry that defines MacArthur. Highlights of the Festival
The Bagacay Festival is known for its lively and colorful events that bring the entire community together. Key features include:

  • Street Dancing Competitions: Energetic dance troupes dressed in vibrant traditional costumes parade through the streets, performing choreographed routines that depict local legends, harvests, and historical events.
  • Cultural Shows and Exhibits: Traditional music, folk dances, and art exhibits that showcase MacArthur’s unique heritage and craftsmanship.
  • Parades and Processions: Religious processions and community parades featuring floats adorned with local symbols, flowers, and traditional motifs.
  • Food Fairs and Local Markets: Authentic Leyteano cuisine and handmade crafts celebrating the town’s culinary and artisanal traditions.
Community Spirit and Tourism
Beyond the festivities, the Bagacay Festival fosters a strong sense of community unity and local pride. It also serves as a welcoming occasion for tourists, providing a wonderful opportunity to immerse oneself in MacArthur’s culture and hospitality. The festival’s timing during the dry season encourages visitors to explore other attractions in the municipality, making it a perfect time for cultural tourism.

When and Where
Date: June 17 (Annual)
Location: Various venues across MacArthur, Leyte — including the town center, main streets, and barangays.

Experience the Heart of MacArthur

The Bagacay Festival is a vibrant showcase of history, culture, and community spirit that invites everyone to celebrate the rich heritage of MacArthur. Whether you’re a local or a visitor, joining the festivities offers a meaningful connection to the town’s past and a joyful glimpse of its present.
