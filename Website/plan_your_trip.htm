HOW DO I GET TO MACARTHUR

There are two main ways to reach Iceland: by plane or ferry. Approximately 20 airlines operate year-round and seasonal flights from Europe and North America to Keflavík International Airport (KEF), Iceland’s primary hub. The Norröna ferry offers a scenic route from Denmark for travelers wishing to bring their own car.

ARRIVAL IN MACARTHUR

Bus services are operated between Reykjavik and Keflavík International Airport in connection with all arriving and departing flights, and taxis are also available. Buses depart as soon as flights arrive at Keflavík. It is recommended to pre-book the bus if arriving late. A 45-minute drive to the capital awaits. From there, you’ll be in the heart of Iceland’s most beloved 2.5-hour route of unmatched adventure. It is possible to arrange private pickup. Taxi services are also available and might cost more. The express MS Jómfrúin sails weekly between Denmark and Seyðisfjörður in East Iceland giving visitors the option of bringing their own vehicle.
